# Genre: Business

Clarity over drama. The reader is busy and skeptical; the design earns trust by being
organized, confident, and quick to yield its point. Flashiness reads as compensation.

## Structure & text

- Every piece leads with its finding. Standfirsts state the takeaway in one sentence;
  headlines are specific ("Why the mid-market is buying again"), never mystery boxes.
- Body text in 3-column spans, tight and efficient. Subheads every 4–6 paragraphs so a
  skimming reader can navigate.
- Sidebars are welcome: a boxed 100–150 word "what this means" or "the numbers" panel per
  feature helps the time-poor reader and breaks the text wall.

## Charts and pull-stats

- Numbers are this genre's imagery. One strong chart or one big pulled stat can be a
  spread's dominant element — often better than a photograph.
- Pull-stats: the number set at display scale, the unit and context set small beside it,
  source line beneath. One stat per treatment; a wall of six stats is a table, so make it
  a table.
- Charts: one message per chart, titled with the finding ("Margins doubled since 2023"),
  not the axis description. Always sourced.

## Photography

- Portrait conventions: executives and founders shot straight, well-lit, in their real
  environment — office, floor, site. Confident but not heroic: no low-angle power shots,
  no arms-crossed-against-skyline clichés.
- Circular crops are acceptable for small repeated portraits (columnists, panel of
  quoted sources) — never for the main feature portrait.
- Workplace/industry photography as evidence: real operations, captioned with specifics.

## Layout temperature

- Confident, not flashy: strong grid, firm rules and dividers, generous but disciplined
  whitespace. Color restraint — the accent color works hardest on charts and stat
  treatments, not decoration.
- One visual peak per feature (the stat, the chart, or the portrait) — not one per spread.

## Pacing

- FOB runs briskest here: market notes, one-number items, short profiles.
- Features alternate analysis spreads (text + sidebar) with evidence spreads (chart or
  stat dominant). End on outlook — a forward-looking closer paragraph, quiet layout.

## Pattern preferences

Favor: `stat-punch`, `data-evidence`, `text-well-three-column`, `portrait-profile`,
`fob-stack`, `sidebar-feature`.
Avoid: `poster-type-opener` (too loud; its arc variant never), full-bleed openers unless
the photograph is genuinely strong reportage of the subject.
