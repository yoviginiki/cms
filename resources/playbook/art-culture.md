# Genre: Art & culture

Images lead; text accompanies. The magazine behaves like a gallery with good wall text.
Design may be expressive — this is the one genre where the layout itself is allowed to
perform, but only on openers, and always in service of the work being shown.

## Artwork reproduction — hard rules

- **Never crop an artwork.** A painting, photograph-as-artwork, sculpture, or installation
  view runs complete, on a quiet ground, with air around it. Cropping is for editorial
  photography, not for the work itself.
- **Always credit:** artist, title, year, medium, and courtesy/photo credit in the caption.
  An uncredited artwork does not run.
- Give artworks a neutral field: white or near-white ground, no gradient overlays, no type
  crossing the work.

## Photography (editorial, not artwork)

- Full-bleed is permitted and encouraged for editorial photography: studio visits,
  portraits of artists, installation atmospheres. Bleed generously; let one image own a
  whole spread when it can carry it.
- Portraits of artists may be characterful — environment, hands, tools — rather than
  formal.

## Text

- Text can be sparse. A spread with one image and eighty words is complete if the image is
  strong. Do not pad.
- Generous whitespace is the genre's signature: wider margins of silence than any other
  genre, single-column text floating in space, captions set small and far from crowding
  the work.
- Body text stays disciplined even when display type plays: standard measure, standard
  leading.

## Experimental type

- Allowed **on openers only**: oversized display, tight cropping of letterforms, rotated
  headlines, text on a path. One gesture per opener — pick the gesture, commit, keep
  everything else plain.
- Never experimental type on body spreads, captions, or credits.

## Pacing

- Alternate immersion and contemplation: a full-bleed spread, then an artwork-on-white
  spread. The white spreads are where the reader actually looks at art; protect them.
- End features on a quiet image, not on text.

## Pattern preferences

Favor: `full-bleed-opener`, `poster-type-opener` (experimental gesture allowed),
`artwork-plate` (the genre's workhorse), `image-grid-quartet` (for series/portfolios),
`quiet-single-column`, `image-interruption`, the `poster-type-opener` arc variant
(openers only).
Avoid: `stat-punch`, `text-well-three-column` (too newsy), tables of any kind.
