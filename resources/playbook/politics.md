# Genre: Politics & current affairs

The register is restraint. The material is serious; the design's job is to get out of its
way and lend it authority. If a layout choice draws attention to itself, it is wrong.

## Photography

- Documentary photography only: real people, real places, available light. No symbolic
  stock imagery — no gavels, no chess pieces, no handshakes in silhouette, no flags
  rippling against sunsets. If no honest image exists for a passage, run text.
- **Never aestheticize suffering.** Images of victims, ruins, or grief are evidence, not
  atmosphere: modest scale, never full-bleed for beauty's sake, never cropped for drama,
  never behind a gradient with type on top.
- Strict caption sourcing: every image caption names who/where/when and, where relevant,
  the photographer or source. An unsourced image does not run.
- Portraits of officials: straight, frontal, unglamorous. No hero angles.

## Typography & text

- Sober typography: regular and medium weights, minimal tracking games, no italic display.
  Headlines are declarative sentences or tight noun phrases — not puns.
- Generous text measure: politics pieces are read, not skimmed. Favor 3-column body spans
  with open leading; long pieces may run 2 wide columns for an essay feel.
- Standfirsts do real work: 2–3 sentences that state the argument, not tease it.

## Evidence blocks

- Data earns space: tables, stat callouts, and timelines are first-class elements here.
  A precise number set large (with its source in the caption position) beats a photo that
  merely gestures at the topic.
- Every chart or table cites its source line beneath it. No source, no chart.

## Pacing

- The well can run denser than other genres — readers of politics tolerate (and trust)
  text-heavy spreads. Still alternate: after a dense argument spread, a documentary photo
  spread with one image and one caption.
- Pull quotes sparingly, and only direct quotations from named people — never the writer's
  own line pulled for drama.

## Pattern preferences

Favor: `text-well-two-column`, `text-well-three-column`, `document-evidence`, `stat-punch`
(numbers only, sourced), `quiet-single-column`, `image-evidence-pair`.
Avoid: `image-grid-quartet` (reads as a mood board), decorative uses of
`full-bleed-opener` (use it only when the photograph is itself reportage), the
`poster-type-opener` arc variant and any `text_path` treatment (never).
